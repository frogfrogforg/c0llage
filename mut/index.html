2021.02.22.1 - More thoughts on thoughts

After using domino for a while, I realised (I hate this word, is it with z or with s??? discord keeps correcting me to use s) that it is still not ideal for a couple of reasons:

  • the size of each block is not very big to contain big thoughts, so I have to force myself to do chunks, which makes me think too much while writing
  • Even if a 2d grid is way more free than like this 1d html document, there is a slight offset and rigidity that makes it hard to actually go to places I want to.
    • there's definitely the possibility to mod it, breaking the grid and creating bezier arrows between things that might make it really nice for my personal use
    • probably for someone more organized it might work better as well.

in the end of the day, dynalist is my tool of choice, which unfortunately is not open source. I think someday I might find someone that uses org mode to show me how they do it and if it gives the same functionality. A wyiwyg thing that has an infinitely nested tree-like structure really goes really well with how I think, especially with the possibility of creating links to other parts of the structure. That and the ability to fold and focus on specific regions is so good. I have to see the export possibilities of dynalist.

I wasn't sure if I wanted to create my own page until now, but reading everyone else I realized it seems actually cool (and a pretty nice way to put our ideas out and try connecting them with the other ideas people seem to be having)

Haven't done much work (other than trying to come up with ideas) for my free salad game yet. I think I kind of connect a little to what ty is mentioning in his own page about this game. I don't totally vibe with the theme. But in the other hand I hardly ever really vibe with a theme. Again a parallel to ty, I pretty much vibe with whatever I am actually vibing at the moment. I also really like how silly it is, and giving a bit more credit, it's a more interesting theme than like 90% of gamejams I can think of (this being a counter example)

And even more so, I really like collaboration, and sometimes I feel that I am some sort of parasite, where I need to have someone else's creative juice flowing in order to maximize my own stuff. Working on this kind of solo kind of group project seems to be a pretty cool challenge for myself.

I've been seeing other people's stuff and kept feeling I wanted to like mess around with everything instead of making my own stuff. Hard to think that I don't mind being invisible in the final thing as long as I personally see I had influence on it? Hopefully in the future I'll be more on the haunting side than on the creating side.
